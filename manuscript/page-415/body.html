'Tis but the flapping of the sail,
And not a rent made by the gale! In spite of rock, and tempest roar,
In spite of false lights on the shore,
Sail on, nor fear to breast the sea! Our hearts, our hopes, are all with thee.
Our hearts, our hopes, our prayers, our tears,
Our faith, triumphant o'er our fears,
Are all with thee, are all with thee!


\-Henry W. Longfellow.

> The Constitution and Laws are here personified, and addressed as "The Ship of State."
